Gas Exchange

What is Gaseous Exchange?


For cellular respiration to take place the respiratory system makes Oxygen available and the byproduct Carbon dioxide is eliminated. Gaseous Exchange is the exchange of Oxygen and Carbon dioxide across a gaseous exchange surface.For this to take place breathing has to take place.Breathing is the mechanical process whereby air moves into and out of the lungs.When air is breathed it is said inhalation/inspration has taken place and when air is is breathed out exhalation/expiration has taken place.


Requirements of an effective gaseous exchange system.

/Requirements Why it is important?
Surface area must be large For maximum diffusion of gases ie. Oxygen and Carbon dioxide
Surface area must be thin. For rapid diffusion of gases ie. Oxygen and Carbon dioxide.
Surface area must be moist. To prevent dissication(drying out) of the gas exchange tissue and so that gases can dissolve to form a solution.
Transport system must be available. To transport gases to and from the gas exchange surfaces.
well ventilating mechanism must be present. To ensure that oxygen-laden air is brought in and Carbon dioxide-laden air is driven out.
Gas exchange surface must be protected. Gas exchange surface are thin and delicate.
